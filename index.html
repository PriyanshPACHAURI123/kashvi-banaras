Our Heritage

Where the Ganga
Meets the Golden Loom

"In every thread lies a prayer. In every weave, a story passed from father to son."

Kashvi Banaras was born from a singular conviction: that the world's most extraordinary textiles deserve to be worn by the world's most extraordinary women. We work exclusively with sixth-generation master weavers in the narrow galis of Varanasi — families who have guarded the secrets of kadhua weaving, kinkhab, and shikargah patterns since the Mughal era.

Each piece is a collaboration between artisan and imagination. Our zari never tarnishes. Our silks are sourced from the finest mulberry farms. And every creation carries a handwritten certificate of authenticity, signed by the weaver who made it.

Our Craft

A Saree is Born in
Twelve Weeks of Prayer

From the first warp thread to the final border seal, each Kashvi Banaras creation passes through the hands of fifteen artisans. What you wear is not merely fabric — it is the distillation of a civilisation's finest art.

01

Silk Sourcing

Only double-threaded mulberry silk from Malda and Mysore enters our looms — chosen by hand.

02

Dyeing

Threads are hand-dyed using mineral pigments in our own ateliers, aged for permanence.

03

Naqsha Design

A master naqshband draws the pattern on graph paper — thousands of squares encoding the weave.

04

Loom Setup

Setting the throw loom takes three days. A single mistake in the warp means starting over.

05

Kadhua Weaving

The weaver works 12 hours a day for weeks, placing each gold thread by hand — two inches per hour.
